Welcome to Yogi Cards!

This is an informative and interactive web app that will teach you more about the many yoga poses that exist, as well as help you memorize them. If you're here, you're likely someone very interested in yoga who may be interested in becoming a yoga teacher one day. Here you'll find some tips and things to know before investing in a yoga teacher training course. In the meantime, read on and play the interactive memory game at the end to test your knowledge on yoga poses!

What to Consider Before Diving Into Yoga Teacher Training

So you've been thinking about teaching yoga someday, huh? Yoga teacher training courses have become widely available, but tuition can range anywhere from $1,000- $4,000. Since they are very pricey, it is common to think long and hard about whether you are willing to make the investment or whether it is even a right fit for you. Here are four signs that you may be ready for Yoga Teacher Training according to Kripalu, a nonprofit educational organization for yoga & health:

  • 1. You're passionate about exploring yoga both on and off the mat.
  • 2. You are willing to continue learning with a beginner's mind, regardless of how advanced you are.
  • 3. You feel driven to share with others the lessons and practices of yoga that have changed your life.
  • 4. You crave an immersive experience to deepen your practice.

If you relate to any of these, you should probably continue searching for the perfect Yoga Teacher Training for you. If you want to keep improving your practice in the meantime, read on to review some key yoga poses using the pose cards and play the interactive memory game.

Asanas

Here is some more in-depth information about some challenging Asanas you will need to know in order to become a yoga teacher.

Information from Yoga Basics, Yoga Journal, and Kripalu Center for Yoga & Health.

Bow Pose
  • Asana: Dhanurasana
  • Form: Begin by lying on the floor, face down. Lift your head and shoulders off the ground. Lift one foot at a time and reach back to grab the top of that foot. Repeat on the other side. Once you have a hold of both feet behind you, contract your glutes and hamstrings to lift your upper body farther off the ground. Keep the crown of your head reaching tall and your back and core muscles engaged to maintain your balance. Hold for 3-6 breaths, then slowly let go to lie back on the floor face down.
  • Purpose: energize body and tonify the kidneys, strengthen back and hamstrings, stretch core
  • Common Mistakes: Not contracting from your legs to bring you higher, not keeping abs and back engaged to maintain balance
Reverse Warrior II
  • Asana: Viparita Virabhadrasana
  • Form: From Warrior II with the right knee bent, cartwheel your arms up and back. Your right arm should be up in the air reaching toward the sky and your left arm will be resting on the back of your left leg. You can either look up or straight ahead. Keep your legs bent and firm, with your feet pressing into the ground. Hold for 2-6 breaths.
  • Purpose: deep side body stretches, opens hips, strengthens lower body
  • Common mistakes: Not sinking deeper into the ground, resting arm on the back of the knee (this can cause injury), not engaging core to parallel back into Warrior II when finished with pose
Firelog Pose
  • Asana: Agnistambhasana
  • Form: Begin in a comfortable seated position. Move the left ankle/foot and place it above your right knee. Move your right foot to be directly under your left knee. Both calves should be parallel to one another. Imagine there is a string that is straightening your torso up toward the sky. Take a deep breath and when you’re ready, slowly hinge at the hips to reach the crown of the head forward toward the ground in front of you. Go as low as you feel comfortable doing and hold for 4-6 breaths.
  • Purpose: Stretches lower back, glutes, and leg muscles. Opens hips. Stimulates abdominal muscles to help with digestion.
  • Common mistakes: Not relaxing your legs to let your knees drop to the ground to open the hips, not hinging at the hips
Plow Pose
  • Asana: Halasana
  • Form: Begin by lying with your back on the mat and your palms down by your sides. Bend your knees, then kick and rock them back to draw your knees to your forehead and your feet to the ground above your head. Keep your arms lengthened on the floor but begin to shuffle your hands together to hold them firmly on the ground below your hips. This helps you stabilize your body. Hold for 4-6 breaths.
  • Purpose: Stimulates blood and lymph circulation, the immune system, and the thyroid. Also helps release stress and tension in the upper body
  • Common Mistakes: Not engaging core to keep your body upright when in position
Extended One-Legged Pigeon Pose
  • Asana name: Utthita Eka Pada Kapotasana
  • Form: From a down dog or plank position, slide knee forward on the ground between your hands and lay your leg on the floor. Your other leg should be stretched out long behind you in a straight line. Hold this pose for a few breaths and then slowly walk yourself back up to switch to the other side.
  • Purpose: deeply opens the hips and groin, intended to be introspective and calm anxiety
  • Common mistakes: Hips not square to the ground, not hinging forward from the hips, not relaxing into position
Revolved Head to Knee Pose
  • Asana: Parivrtta Janu Sirsasana
  • Form: From a comfortable seated position, stretch out the right leg at a 45 degree angle. Bend the left leg to bring the left foot in towards you. Reach your right hand over your right thigh, with your right palm facing up. Next, reach your left hand over your torso to reach over for your foot as well. Hold 4-6 breaths.
  • Purpose: Opens hips, deeply stretches side body
  • Common mistakes: Not opening the chest by turning the torso outward, not going slowly so as not to hurt yourself.
Dolphin Pose
  • Asana: Ardha Pincha Mayurasana
  • Form: From table top position, lower your elbows and forearms to the ground while keeping the palms of your hands firmly on the ground. Tuck your toes and lift your hips up toward the sky. Hold for 4-6 breaths.
  • Purpose: Stimulates nervous system, strengthens upper body, stretches lower body and spine.
  • Common Mistakes: Not keeping spine, head, and neck in a straight line while upside down. Not hinging at the hips.
Half Pyramid Pose
  • Asana: Ardha Parsvottanasana
  • Form: From right leg lunge position, retract your body backwards to sit on the heel of your left foot. Your right leg will be stretched out in front of you. Make sure you keep a small bend in the right knee, but hinge forward from the hips to get the proper hamstring stretch. Hold for 4-10 breaths and then alternate sides.
  • Purpose: Hamstring stretch, calms nervous system.
  • Common mistakes: Not hinging from hips to extend forward, not keeping a bend in the knees, not keeping torso in a line rather than slouching.
Four Limbed Staff Pose
  • Asana: Chaturanga
  • Form: Hold your body in a pushup position. Your core and quads should be engaged to hold your head, torso, and legs in a straight line. Your elbows should be bent at a 90 degree angle. The crown of your head will be forward and the toes tucked pressing the heels back. Hold for 1-4 breaths.
  • Purpose: strengthen lower back, upper body, and core
  • Common Mistakes: Elbows not at 90 degrees, hips not tucked leading to abs that aren’t engaged, crown of head not forward
Seated Spinal Twist Pose
  • Asana: Matsyendrasana
  • Form: Sit upright on your mat with your back straight and legs outstretched in front of you. Lift one knee up and draw to your chest. Lead with your opposite side to turn into your knee. Stretch that arm to hug your knee close into your chest and hold for a few breaths.
  • Purpose: Lengthens, strengthens, and nourishes spine. Stimulants nervous, reproductive, and digestive systems.
  • Common mistakes: Not straightening back enough, not stretching deeper into it with every breath
Fish Pose
  • Asana: Matsyasana
  • Form: Begin by lying on the floor with your back and palms to the ground. Slide your hands under your glutes keeping a slight bend in the elbows. Press into the elbows and use your arms to begin arching your spine to peel your chest off the ground while keeping the crown of your head touching the ground. You should be using your arms, torso, legs, and glutes to support your body NOT your head or your neck. Hold for 4-6 breaths.
  • Purpose: Stimulates cardiovascular, respiratory, and nervous systems; helps reduce anxiety and opens chest.
  • Common mistakes: Using head/neck to support your body, not keeping legs long on the ground
Half Moon Pose
  • Asana: Ardha Chandrasana
  • Form: From mountain pose, find a stable point on the floor or a few feet in front of you and begin hinging at the hips to lift the left leg back while moving the torso forward to straighten. Reach your left hand toward the ground while keeping your body in line to the best of your ability. Try to maintain your balance by engaging your core and keeping your sight focused on that stable point. Reach your right arm to the sky and look forward. Hold for 4-6 breaths.
  • Purpose: Improves balance, focus, and concentration. Opens hips and strengthens lower body.
  • Common Mistakes: Not engaging core to keep balance.
